# Notes vault

## Get started

1. **Install Obsidian** — Download the app from [obsidian.md/download](https://obsidian.md/download).
2. **Clone this repo** (pick a parent folder you like):

   ```bash
   git clone https://github.com/saisritejakuppaEros/Notes.git
   cd Notes
   ```

3. **Open the vault** — Launch Obsidian → **Open folder as vault** → choose the cloned `Notes` folder (the one that contains this `README.md` and `.obsidian`).

This vault is organized around the **ACE framework** from Nick Milo ([Linking Your Thinking](https://www.linkingyourthinking.com/)): a note layout for Obsidian built on **links**, not only nested folders.

## What ACE is

**ACE** stands for **Atlas**, **Calendar**, and **Efforts** — three “headspaces” for how you work with knowledge:

| Headspace | Orienting lens | Purpose | Guiding question |
|-----------|----------------|---------|------------------|
| **Atlas** | Knowledge | Understand — ideas, concepts, Maps of Content (MOCs) | Where would you like to go? |
| **Calendar** | Time | Focus — daily notes, logs, reflections on a date | What’s on your mind? |
| **Efforts** | Action | Act — projects and work by importance / energy | What can you work on? |





# Knowledge Management Structure

The system is divided into three main folders, each serving a different purpose.

## Atlas

Atlas is the map of everything I'm working on. Since I tend to explore many different fields and projects at the same time, it helps me keep track of all my areas of work and understand the bigger picture. Whenever I wonder _"What am I currently working on?"_, Atlas is the place to look.

---

## Calendars

Calendars contain my daily work logs. These are not organized by project but by date. Each entry briefly records what I worked on, the key outcomes, how I spent my time, and the plan for the next day. This helps me measure whether I met my expectations and keeps me accountable to my daily goals.

---

## Efforts

Efforts contain the detailed documentation of my work. This is where I record my thought process, experiments, decisions, implementation details, and progress while working on a project.

The **Efforts** folder is divided into three sections:

- **Ongoing** – Projects that I'm actively working on.
    
- **Sleeping** – Projects that are currently paused but may be revisited in the future.
    
- **Notes** – Once an ongoing project becomes stable and the knowledge is well organized, it is moved here as a clean reference for future use.
    

In short:

- **Atlas** → Know **what** I'm working on.
    
- **Calendars** → Know **when** I worked on it, **how much time** I spent, and plan the next day.
    
- **Efforts** → Know **how** the work was done through detailed documentation.
